DAY THREE

How is my commission calculated for hair services versus other beauty services? 
Hair services have a "performance-trigger" rate: you earn a 20% commission if your total hair service amount reaches ₱6,000 or more. If you fall below that quota, the commission is 10%. For nails, lashes, and waxing, the commission is a flat 10% of the original service price regardless of the total.
What is the primary goal of this commission structure? 
According to management, the system is designed to motivate staff to upsell services and work as a team to hit targets that benefit both the individual and the business.
Why is there a daily team goal of ₱8,000? 
This is a "break-even" target. It ensures the shop generates enough revenue to cover essential operational expenses, including rent, electricity, water, and professional supplies.
Does the daily goal affect my individual pay? 
While the ₱8,000 is a collective team target, hitting higher numbers contributes to the Weekly Tiered Commission, which increases everyone's payout percentage.

If commissions are computed monthly, why do we receive them weekly? 
Management computes the totals monthly to ensure 100% accuracy in the sales data, but they distribute the funds weekly to provide staff with consistent cash flow.
What is the policy regarding tardiness? 
There is a 15-minute grace period. If you arrive more than 15 minutes late, a deduction of ₱1 per minute is applied. Note that "early outs" (leaving before the shift ends) are also subject to deductions.
When are attendance-related deductions taken out of my pay? 
All deductions for tardiness or early departures are consolidated and applied during the payroll of the last week of the month.
Am I eligible for a cash advance? 
Cash advance privileges are not guaranteed; they are strictly dependent on your attendance record. Employees with poor punctuality or frequent absences may be denied this privilege.
What other deductions should I expect on my payslip? 
Aside from tardiness, deductions may include payments for loans or uniform fees, which are regulated by management to ensure the system remains fair for all employees.
